InterviewPrep is designed to provide you with the proper tools and techniques to prepare for both face-to-face and virtual interviews. Explore InterviewPrep now for free, for additional practice and tips on how to be successful during an interview.





For better preparation review these tips before the big day:


Review common interview questions.

Some of the most common interview questions that you will face include:

  • Strengths/Weaknesses
  • Willingness to learn new things
  • Work and flexibility
  • Leadership skills
  • Problem solving ability
  • Work and volunteer contributions

Bring important items.

  • Portfolio
  • Extra copies of your resume and list of references
  • Copies of letter(s) of recommendation, licenses, transcripts, etc.

On the day of the interview remember to.

  • Arrive 10 to 15 minutes early
  • Go by yourself
  • Dress professional
  • Turn off your cellphone
  • Leave all miscellaneous items in the car

Use the measures to display confidence.

  • Shake hand firmly
  • Maintain eye contact
  • Listen carefully, and welcome all questions even the difficult ones
  • Give honest and direct answers
  • Develop answers in your head before responding
  • Ask for clarity on the question, or have the question repeated if you don't understand the question

Remember: Don't rush, you don't want to appear indecisive.

How to end the interview.

  • Be courteous and allow the interview to end on time
  • Restate strengths and experiences, that you may have not emphasized earlier
  • Mention any accomplishments or activities that pretain to the job
  • Find out if there will be any additional interviews
  • Indicate a time for when you may contact the employer to learn about the future decision
  • Remember to send a thank you note or letter after the interview
